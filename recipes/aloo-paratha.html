Aloo Paratha

Description

Aloo Paratha is a popular and delectable Indian flatbread that originates from the North Indian cuisine. This dish features a soft, whole wheat flour dough stuffed with a spiced filling of mashed potatoes, onions, and various aromatic spices. Aloo Paratha is a versatile and hearty dish enjoyed during breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • Salt (to taste)

For the potato filling:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Ghee or oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, a pinch of salt, and water gradually.
    2. Knead the mixture into a soft and smooth dough.
    3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the potato filling:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the mashed potatoes with chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, cumin seeds, garam masala, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
    2. Optionally, add chopped fresh coriander leaves for extra flavor.
    3. Mix everything well to form a uniform filling.
  3. Assemble and roll the parathas:
    1. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls, slightly smaller than a golf ball.
    2. Roll each ball into a small disc and place a portion of the potato filling in the center.
    3. Bring the edges of the dough together to seal the filling and form a stuffed ball.
    4. Roll out each stuffed ball into a flat, round paratha using a rolling pin.
  4. Cook the aloo parathas:
    1. Heat a griddle or a tawa over medium-high heat.
    2. Place a rolled paratha on the hot griddle and cook until bubbles appear on the surface.
    3. Flip the paratha, apply a little ghee or oil on the cooked side, and cook until both sides are golden brown.
    4. Repeat the process for the remaining parathas.
  5. Serve the hot Aloo Parathas with yogurt, pickles, or a side of your choice.

Enjoy your homade aloo parathas!
